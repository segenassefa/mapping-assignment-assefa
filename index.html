Brooklyn's Chinatown: The Chinese-American Community of Bensonhurst

By Segen Assefa

December 8, 2021

"Brooklyn, New York" by Dougtone is licensed under CC BY-SA 2.0

Over the last decade, the Chinese population of Bensonhurst has appeared to grow, with small, predominantly Chinese communities in locations like 86th Street and Bay Ridge Parkway. What used to be a predominantly Italian neighborhood has now become a melting pot of Chinese, Latino, Middle Eastern, and Eastern European residents.

"Many Chinese residents are attracted to Bensonhurst because of it's location and lower living costs, compared to places like Sunset Park and the Lower East Side," says Dr. Tim Law, the head organizer at the Chinese American Social Services Center on Bay Parkway. Law immigrated to New York in his twenties, and has lived in Bensonhurst for over 40 years.

But the numbers tell a different story.

As the population of Bensonhurst grows, with 186,959 residents as of 2019, the numbers in the Chinese community have fallen ever so slightly. However, while the numbers support this theory, the decrease in the Chinese community of Bensonhurst has been slight, only dropping 2.7% in the last 7 years. As the numbers decrease slightly, more and more of the Chinese residents of Bensonhurst are moving into the surrounding communities, with some even travelling as far as Long Island.
